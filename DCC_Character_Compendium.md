# MAIN CHARACTERS

## Carl (Crawler Carl)
- **Species/Race:** Human → Primal
- **Role:** Crawler (protagonist/narrator)
- **First Appearance:** Book 1
- **Books:** 1–7
- **Class Evolution:** Pugilist → Compensated Anarchist → Agent Provocateur → Primal Pugilist
- **Visual Reference (Book 7 / Final Appearance):** White American male, late 20s, tall and physically strong (Coast Guard veteran build). Clean-shaven face (shaves daily). Long, wavy, luscious hair kept in a ponytail tucked under his cloak — the result of a hair tonic mishap after being burned bald at the end of Book 6. Described as looking "romance novel cover-like" and compared to Fabio. Closely resembles his father (same nose). **Face:** Small lizard-foot/gecko tattoo just below his left eye, barely noticeable, like a tiny teardrop freckle. **Left arm:** Large "ball of snakes" Enemy of the Church tattoo; faded green-tinted spiderweb tattoo on the elbow (poorly drawn, worm-like segmented strands, "prison tattoo" style). **Right forearm:** Shining new tattoo (chain-shaped dragon, slightly crooked). **Neck:** Desperado Club skull tattoo. **Left inner forearm:** Goblin Pass brand. **Chest:** Eye of the Bedlam Bride tattoo (invisible when the eye is closed; opens and blinks when activated). **Hands:** Glow from Emberus/Heathen Burn markings. **Outfit (top to bottom):** Nightgaunt Cloak with hood (outermost layer); "The Enchanted Anarchist's Battle Rattle" — a sleeveless leather jacket covered in sewn-on patches (five small patches on the front/shoulders: earth symbol, arrows, bomb, peace symbol, Midnight Epicure; one large back patch depicting a pyramid of skulls from various creatures with a glowing centipede crawling through them, stitched with fiberoptic-like wire). Under the jacket: a form-fitting, cool-to-touch short-sleeved Trollskin Shirt. **Lower body:** White "BigBoi Boxers" covered in little red hearts (enchanted, self-sizing) — **no pants** (Carl famously never wears pants through the entire series). Enchanted Spiked Kneepads of the Shade Gnoll Riot Forces (slip-on bracers with retractable needle-like spikes). **Feet:** Barefoot (has tactical boots but only wears them in safe rooms). Enchanted Toe Ring of the Splatter Skunk on one foot. **Right hand/arm:** Charcoal-colored War Gauntlet of the Exalted Grull (a bracer that transforms into a spiked war gauntlet of orcish steel when he makes a fist). Overall silhouette: a tall, muscular, bare-legged man in heart-patterned boxers and spiked kneepads, wearing a patched sleeveless leather jacket under a hooded cloak, with a glowing back patch, long ponytail, multiple visible tattoos, and a spiked gauntlet on one fist.
- **Description:** Carl is a 27-year-old former US Coast Guard member turned marine technician who survives the apocalyptic collapse of Earth's surface while wearing nothing but boxers, a leather jacket, and pink Crocs. He becomes one of the dungeon's most famous crawlers alongside his cat Princess Donut. Pragmatic, sarcastic, and protective, Carl is a brilliant improviser specializing in explosives — his signature "Carl's Jug O' Boom" firebombs and increasingly sophisticated IEDs become his trademark. He chooses the Primal race on Floor 3 and the Compensated Anarchist class, later specializing as an Agent Provocateur. His abilities include Protective Shell, Daughter's Kiss (a devastating stomp/punch attack), Gloom Wraith Phase, bomb crafting, and xistera throwing. Across the series he levels from 1 to 81, accumulates tattoos (Emberus martyr, Enemy of the Church, Shi Maria eye, dragon chain), worships the fire god Emberus, and becomes increasingly radicalized against the dungeon system itself. His ex-girlfriend Beatrice owned Donut; she is revealed alive in Book 5. In Book 6 he discovers he has a half-brother named Asher. By Book 7 he leads the Princess Posse faction in the Faction Wars, assassinates King Rust, frees the dungeon AI by deactivating the Containment Interface, and wins the war.

## Princess Donut (GC, BWR, NW Princess Donut the Queen Anne Chonk)
- **Species/Race:** Tortoiseshell Persian Cat (Enhanced)
- **Role:** Crawler, Carl's partner, party leader
- **First Appearance:** Book 1
- **Books:** 1–7
- **Class Evolution:** Former Child Actor → Glass Cannon → Legendary Diva (Bard) → Deckmaster → Deathbed Hellcat → The Oak Fell / Champion of Nekhebit
- **Visual Reference (Book 7 / Final Appearance):** A fluffy, flat-faced tortoiseshell Persian cat — the kind that looks like it should be sitting on a Bond villain's lap. Black, beige, and white fur coloring with a mottled pattern visible on shaved skin. Large, expressive yellow eyes. Squished/flat nose (too flat to hold glasses without magic). Long, fluffy tail used expressively (curls around Carl's neck, swishes when agitated). Fur poofs out dramatically when scared or angry. Small enough to sit on Carl's shoulder or ride Mongo, but heavier than she looks due to massively increased Strength stats. Moves with panther-like grace, flowing like liquid across the floor. **Head:** The Tiara of a Thousand Lights — daintier and prettier than her original crown, covered in approximately 100 tiny jewels each sparkling a different color, catching light like a brilliant diamond, glowing like a lighthouse beacon. (35 of 99 absorption slots filled with absorbed hat skills including Perfect Night Vision.) **Body:** Cat-sized ghillie suit of color-changing, shredded fabric that blends into the background (covers entire body, worn as jacket slot — creates strange contrast with the glowing tiara). Over this, the Cloak of the Benevolent Champion — silky fabric, black on one side and blue on the other, almost like a magician's cape but with short arm-holes making it more robe-like, glowing with soft white light. **Rear half:** Enchanted Fae Scale Quadruped Crupper — silver scale armor made of Fae Steel draped over the back half of her body reaching halfway to the floor, like a metallic skirt with a slot for her tail. Jingles and clinks when she moves, poofs out like a skirt during combat. **Back:** Quadruped weapon mount carrying a large crossbow bigger than Donut herself, forming with an ominous click (Katia's crossbow, "the scorpion's bite"). **Face:** Prism Industries Special High-Fashion "Princess Donut" Goggles — large, round, bug-like sunglasses with no arms, magically affixed to her squished face (reminiscent of movie-star sunglasses). **Legs:** Fallen Oak anklet with three little rattling beads on one back leg; various rings and bracelet on front leg. **Right flank:** Small, round bald patch with an animated line-art tattoo of a dog (moves on its own — lifts its leg, licks itself), overlaid with glowing blue and purple Words of Power. She refuses to acknowledge this tattoo exists. Overall silhouette: a fluffy, flat-faced Persian cat wearing a brilliant multi-jeweled tiara, enormous round sunglasses, a color-shifting camouflage suit under a glowing black-and-blue cape, jingling silver scale rear armor, and a crossbow mount on her back that's bigger than she is — equal parts absurd and terrifying.
- **Description:** Originally Beatrice's pampered show cat, Donut gains sapience and speech via an Enhanced Pet Biscuit on the first floor. A flat-faced tortoiseshell Persian with black, beige, and white coloring, she wears the Enchanted Crown of the Sepsis Whore (a jeweled tiara with purple stones) and later a metallic crupper that jingles like a skirt. Donut is vain, dramatic, attention-loving, fiercely loyal, and surprisingly shrewd — her Charisma stat eventually exceeds 300. She types in ALL CAPS in chat, names NPCs after TV characters, and writes the Princess Posse fanclub newsletter. Her Magic Missile reaches level 15+, and she gains devastating spells including War Crime (an Atrocity-class attack), Clockwork Triplicate (creates copies of Mongo), Second Chance (necromancy), and numerous deity powers from her Oak Fell class. She rides Mongo into battle, adopts him as her "son," and maintains a complicated flirtation with Ferdinand (Gravy Boat). By Book 7 she reaches level 135, is named Champion of Nekhebit, kills Huanxin Jinx with Mordecai's crossbow bolt containing his mother's ashes, and swallows the Ring of Divine Suffering.

## Mordecai (Guildmaster Mordecai)
- **Species/Race:** Changeling (changes form each floor: Skyfowl → Rat Hooligan → Incubus → Grulke Infantry Toad → Rock Edge Skyfowl/Eagle → Pocket Kuma bear → Skyfowl → Human Canadian)
- **Role:** NPC Game Guide / Manager
- **First Appearance:** Book 1
- **Books:** 1–7
- **Visual Reference (All Forms — Final form is Human/Canadian, Book 7):** Mordecai changes form every floor due to his Changeling race. **True form (never seen in-story):** Centurion Skyfowl — a golden eagle-like creature with angel-like wings folded on its back, darker feathers, large wingspan, and big talons. **Book 1–2 (Rat Hooligan):** Master Splinter-like rat creature, short (can't reach shelves), furry hands, wearing a black vest, blue pants, and well-worn sandals. **Book 2 (Incubus):** Stunning transformation — tall (Carl's height), well-built, disgustingly handsome young man with dusky gray skin, short devil horns jutting from his forehead, long gray-and-black hair held back in a ponytail, a barbed tail, and black bat-like wings folded against his back. Wears a tuxedo with slits for his wings. Deep voice. Well-chiseled face with smoldering eyes. Called "the Gigachad of the Over City." **Book 3 (Grulke Infantry/Toad):** About 5 feet tall, mud-colored, warty, slimy toad creature with hanging jowls and an inflatable throat pouch under a wide face. Whip-like tongue that can launch objects at incredible speed. **Book 4 (Rock Edge Skyfowl/Eagle):** Light brown and beige eagle, no arms (just feet and wings like a regular eagle), clipped wings (can only fly short distances — "imagine if you suddenly woke up in the body of a eunuch"), razor-sharp talons that glint like knives, feathers that ruffle around his neck. **Book 5 (Pocket Kuma):** Knee-height, fuzzy, brown and white, chubby bipedal bear thing with elf ears, a monkey tail, ridiculously huge bug-eyes, and buck teeth. Absurdly high-pitched cute voice. Can barely reach doorknobs. Compared to "a bootleg Monchhichi with a bad haircut." **Book 6 (Hills Cyclops):** Same height as when he was an incubus but now with a wide belly stretching his white shirt to its limits. Bright pink skin, bald cone-shaped furrowed forehead, single brown eye the size of an ostrich egg, caterpillar-like eyebrow stretching across his head, wrinkly sausage fingers. Compared to Patrick from SpongeBob. Sounds stupid when he talks. No depth perception, can't see his own reflection. **Book 7 / Final Form (Human/Canadian):** A nerdy-looking balding human man wearing a snowsuit, raccoon hat, and glasses. Compared to Rick Moranis. Says "aboot" instead of "about." Has a well-worn Tim Hortons loyalty card in his Velcro wallet. Obsessed with ice hockey and semi-erotic lumberjack fan fiction (per the AI's race description). When he removes the raccoon hat, a balding head is revealed underneath.
- **Description:** Mordecai is a former crawler who made it to the 11th floor and chose to become a game guide rather than die. He takes a different form each floor due to his Changeling race — notably a disgustingly handsome incubus (Book 2), a warty toad (Book 3), a cute pocket bear with huge eyes (Book 5), and finally a human in Canadian stereotypical garb (Book 7). Level 50 non-combatant NPC. He is snarky, world-weary, an expert alchemist/mycologist, and deeply protective of Carl and Donut despite his grouchy exterior. His traumatic past involves Odette, Chaco, and his brother Uzzi — Huanxin Jinx's actions led to his brother's death, and he carries an urn of his mother's ashes. In Book 2 Donut tricks him into becoming their permanent manager. He attacks Chaco on sight in Book 3. By Book 7 he performs surgery on Louis (heart/lung transplant), and loads his mother's ashes into a crossbow bolt that Donut uses to kill Huanxin Jinx, avenging his brother.

## Katia Grim (Katia Grimmsdottir)
- **Species/Race:** Human → Doppelganger
- **Role:** Crawler, party member
- **First Appearance:** Book 2
- **Books:** 2–7
- **Class:** Monster Truck Driver (HAZMAT endorsement)
- **Visual Reference (Book 7 / True Form Revealed):** Katia's appearance is complicated by her Doppelganger nature — she can reshape her body like clay. **Default "human" disguise (Books 2–7):** A blonde-haired woman (originally with hair too thick, like doll hair, and a poorly sculpted face — eyes too big and too high, crooked sideways nose, lumpy chin). Over time she improves her sculpting: by Book 3 she looks "like someone who'd had too much cosmetic surgery" rather than a burn victim. Eva taunts her about "ugly brown hair" and a "piggy nose." She wears a slick black bodysuit, knee-high boots, and sometimes sports a purple mohawk. Can grow four arms, extra eyes on stalks, extend her tongue into a tiny hand giving the middle finger. **Combat form ("She-Hulk"):** About 8 feet tall and wide, pink-skinned, with spikes on her back. Leather outfit, black boots, purple mohawk, riot shield on left arm, crossbow over one shoulder. Can also form herself into objects (train cowcatcher, diving bell, chair). **True body (Book 7 finale):** When she reverts to her real form, she shrinks dramatically. Black hair (not blonde — the blonde was always the disguise). Small frame. Missing her entire right arm to the shoulder, missing her left leg entirely, missing her right leg below the knee, and missing an ear — injuries accumulated across the series, hidden by her shapeshifting. She waves goodbye with a cybernetic right arm that Bautista built for her. Her true appearance is that of a small, battered Icelandic woman who has given far more of herself physically than anyone realized.
- **Description:** An Icelandic woman with a scarred/burned face from entering the dungeon, Katia joins Carl and Donut's party on Floor 3 after being sent by Hekla. She becomes a Doppelganger with extraordinary shapeshifting abilities — she can grow extra eyes, form herself into a train cowcatcher, diving bell, or disguises, and widen her body mass. Quiet, brave, and dry-humored, she grows from a timid under-leveled crawler into "probably the second most dangerous crawler in the dungeon." She kills Hekla in Book 3 when she discovers she's being used. In Book 5 she kills Eva Sigrid but is cursed with the Crown of the Sepsis Whore. She falls in love with Daniel Bautista. In Book 7 her true physical state is revealed — missing her right arm, left leg, right leg below the knee, and an ear (hidden by dungeon enhancements). She devises the plan to become pregnant (via Louis as ceremonial father) to be teleported to the kinder facility to protect 120,000 children, and disappears to the surface.

# MAJOR RECURRING CHARACTERS

## Mongo
- **Species/Race:** Mongoliensis (Velociraptor)
- **Role:** Pet/companion (Donut's)
- **First Appearance:** Book 1
- **Books:** 1–7
- **Visual Reference (Book 7 / Full-Grown):** At full size (level 15+), Mongo stands pony-height, coming up to just below Carl's shoulders. From beak tip to tail end he is about 13 feet long, though more than half is tail. His tail is long and colorful with red and blue feathers sprouting from the back. His body is covered in colorful feathers — pink-hued downy feathers from infancy gave way to longer, thicker arm feathers mixing deep red with the original pink, and blue-toned skin around his eyes. He has a wide beak that opens to reveal rows of sharp, ripping teeth circling the interior. Two forearms (not wings) with hooked claws. The individual claws on his feet are now bigger than his entire body was as a baby. In Book 7 he is missing one wing (burned off by Shi Maria in Book 6, regrowing with scars after a potion). He wears a saddle matching his feather coloring for Donut to ride. At baby stage (Book 1) he was seven inches tall, looked like "a raw piece of chicken with a few random pink-hued feathers," with a serpentine tail and beady reptilian eyes. He grew through turkey-sized (level 6), waist-high (level 10), to full raptor at level 15.
- **Description:** A baby velociraptor chosen from a pet reward room, Mongo starts as a seven-inch-tall pink-feathered chicken-like creature and grows to full raptor size by level 15. Perpetually aggressive and food-motivated, he bites everything in sight (including Donut's nose constantly). Donut rides him into battle, and he serves as both combat mount and scout (via Clockwork Triplicate copies). He mates with Kiwi (a wild Mongoliensis/bear) and becomes father to twin ursensus babies. In Book 7 he is credited with killing Epitome Tagg and Empress D'Nadia.

## Zev
- **Species/Race:** Kua-tin (aquatic fish-humanoid)
- **Role:** Borant Corporation liaison / entertainment agent
- **First Appearance:** Book 1
- **Books:** 1–7
- **Visual Reference:** About two feet tall — barely reaches Carl's knee. A humanoid fish creature hidden inside a white mesh spacesuit that is half space-age, half old-school deep diving suit. Wears a round glass helmet filled with bubbling water (like a portable fish tank), with tubes snaking from the back of the helmet to a bulky backpack. Her voice is amplified through a small speaker on the suit. She has fish arms and a fish body (no catfish whiskers, unlike her colleague Cascadia). Appears and disappears with a "pop and splash of water." Described repeatedly as tiny — when Donut rubs against her, the small fish creature struggles to keep upright. Often carries a small info pad. She looks increasingly tired and stressed as the series progresses.
- **Description:** About two feet tall, wearing a round glass helmet filled with water and a mesh spacesuit, Zev is Carl and Donut's assigned Borant outreach representative. She bonds with Donut over their shared love of Gossip Girl. Initially professional and cold, she becomes genuinely invested in their survival. She is temporarily replaced by the hostile Loita (Book 4) and later promoted when Borant loses control of the crawl to Valtay brain worms. Throughout the series she manages their show appearances, sponsorships, and provides crucial intel about galactic politics.

## Odette
- **Species/Race:** Unknown alien (legless former crawler)
- **Role:** Talk show host / game guide / behind-the-scenes operator
- **First Appearance:** Book 1
- **Books:** 1–7
- **Visual Reference:** Two distinct appearances. **On-camera costume (her crawler armor):** Lower body is entirely a king-crab shape — black and red with a lumpy shell and multiple chitinous legs, size of a brown bear, legs crowded and twitching. Head covered by a triangular praying mantis mask — black with mirrored compound eyes the size of footballs and twin antennae with a six-foot span. Torso: naked, ebony, plus-sized human model proportions from stomach to neck, with absurdly colossal breasts that rest on the desk surface. Human-like arms with long fake curved nails painted blue. **True form (unmasked):** An approximately sixty-year-old woman. Eyes "just a little wide for her face to pass as someone from earth." Dark shirt and a necklace with a heart on it. No legs — her body stops at her stomach. She floats on a flat magical disk/wheelchair that emanates a slight buzzing sound. The entire crab body, bug mask, and prosthetic chest are her actual armor from when she was a crawler — what she was wearing when she reached the 13th floor stairwell.
- **Description:** A former crawler who lost her legs on the 11th floor, Odette floats on a magical disk and appears on camera as a "naked crab-taur wearing a bug mask" — her lower body disguised as a giant crab body with a praying mantis head mask. She hosts "Dungeon Crawler After Hours with Odette," providing crucial strategic advice to Carl and Donut. Smart, cunning, and self-serving but genuinely helpful. She was Mordecai's trainer during his season. In Book 5 she reveals that Beatrice is alive. In Book 6's prologue she is revealed as Mordecai's original game guide on the 10th floor, managing the plan to kill the goddess Dodola. She orchestrates operations on Earth's surface and employs Lexis and Zev.

## Elle McGib (Mrs. McGibbons)
- **Species/Race:** Human → Frost Maiden → Fairy
- **Role:** Crawler
- **First Appearance:** Book 1
- **Books:** 1–7
- **Class Evolution:** Blizzardmancer → Tundra Princess → Hailstorm Queen
- **Visual Reference (Book 7 — Fairy/Hailstorm Queen):** Originally a wheelchair-bound elderly woman, now transformed into a fairy. About 4.5 feet tall with light blue sky-colored skin. White, haphazardly-cut hair (same messy style as before but fitting her smaller frame). Floats about a foot off the ground. Still wears anti-slip socks from the Meadow Lark retirement home. A privacy bubble around her head like a nimbus/halo, reminiscent of haloed figures in Renaissance paintings. By later books she has fairy wings (confirmed when Samantha threatens to "pull her fairy wings right off"). Hands glow when casting ice magic; shards of snow fly when she claps. Her touch feels like "being wrapped by a pair of icicles." Carries an ice staff. Has pin-up t-shirt merchandise of herself in-universe.
- **Description:** Originally a wheelchair-bound, dementia-suffering elderly woman from the Meadow Lark Adult Care Community, Elle transforms into a Frost Maiden — shrinking to 4.5 feet tall with light blue skin and white hair, floating a foot off the ground. She becomes one of the dungeon's most powerful crawlers with ice-based attacks, eventually evolving into a fairy with wings and the Hailstorm Queen class. Crass, loud, fearless, and a heavy drinker, she provides critical air support and scout capabilities throughout the series. She still wears her anti-slip socks from Meadow Lark.

## Imani C.
- **Species/Race:** Human (transformed — winged, skull-faced)
- **Role:** Crawler
- **First Appearance:** Book 1
- **Books:** 1–7
- **Visual Reference (Book 3 onward — Obsidian Butterfly):** Gaunt, skeletal frame — she was a skinny 90-pound woman before transformation and now looks like she weighs half that. A white skull is painted on her face (reminiscent of Day of the Dead makeup or the Danger Dingos). Most striking are four translucent, ethereal butterfly wings trailing behind her with a roughly 12-foot wingspan — a striking mix of saturated orange, yellow, red, white, and black, like an intensified monarch butterfly pattern. The wings are not physical — they pass through objects and people, but those they pass through briefly glow with a subtle light (providing buffs). She hovers/flies a foot off the ground. About 20 years old. Has 12 player-killer skulls over her head. Wears scrubs and a magical cloak (early books) that evolve into more combat-appropriate gear.
- **Description:** A 20-year-old night staff worker at the Meadow Lark eldercare facility, Imani was forced to mercy-kill dying residents on the first floor, earning player-killer skulls (eventually 12). Very thin, originally about 90 pounds, she transforms into a gaunt skull-faced winged creature. A healer class with practical, protective, maternal instincts, she becomes one of Carl's most reliable allies and enters the stairwell to Floor 9 with him as the party healer.

## Prepotente ("Pony" / Prepotente Lorenzo De La Rosa)
- **Species/Race:** Caprid (sentient goat)
- **Role:** Crawler (originally Miriam's pet)
- **First Appearance:** Book 2
- **Books:** 2–7
- **Class:** Forsaken Aerialist → Profane Vitiate
- **Visual Reference:** A walking, talking goat (Caprid race). Has human-like fingers, though his fingernails are long, curled, and black. His transformation to sapience was different from Donut's — he retains a fully goat-like body and head. Speaks with a nasally British accent, like a spoiled prince. Prone to random short screaming bursts (a goat behavior). Screams when hugged or startled. Travels with his hellspawn familiar goat companion — a huge, dark beast the size of a horse, covered in phantom black flames, with three pairs of human-like breasts running down its underside.
- **Description:** A walking, talking goat who gained sapience like Donut via an enhanced pet biscuit. Extremely arrogant with a nasally British accent, prone to random screaming and casual murder threats. Despite his volatile temperament, he's a genius-level analyst who can deduce entire floor structures from item descriptions. His class stacks debuffs on targets without them realizing. Deeply attached to his mother Miriam Dom (his original shepherd). Reaches level 100 by Book 7. In Book 5's epilogue he shatters the entire 7th floor, bypassing it completely through brilliant deduction.

## Signet (Tsarina Signet)
- **Species/Race:** Half-Naiad, Half High-Elf Summoner
- **Role:** Elite NPC
- **First Appearance:** Book 2
- **Books:** 2, 5, 6
- **Visual Reference:** About 5.5 feet tall, extra-thin humanoid. Long, pointed elf ears. Short horns jutting from her forehead. Sharpened fangs. Skin gives off a mild luminescence. Almost completely naked (wearing only "this quite lovely thong" per Donut). From toes to face, covered in thick-lined, old-school tattoos of Asian and nautical-themed monsters — Japanese Oni, small Chinese dragons, sharks, octopuses, and a three-headed ogre sharpening a curved sword. These tattoos are alive and moving: they twirl around each other, ripple her skin, pause to look at observers and silently hiss before wrapping around her body. A sea dragon pulses across her small breasts. Her face is ghoulish and demon-like — she favors the more monstrous visage of her naiad heritage rather than the beautiful side. Despite the disturbing face, she has a surprisingly seductive voice that doesn't match her appearance. Charisma 100+ (gives her the "Puppy Dog" buff affecting men). Has a spell that keeps rain off her body.
- **Description:** About 5.5 feet tall, extra-thin with long pointed elf ears, short forehead horns, sharpened fangs, and strange moving tattoos. Level 60 Summoner who can summon Blood and Ink Elementals using blood-powered spells. Almost naked, with a malevolent appearance that belies her surprising kindness. She leads armies of forest NPCs and is half-sister to Queen Imogen. She fights alongside Carl in the circus battles (Book 2) and the Butcher's Masquerade (Book 5).

## Daniel Bautista
- **Species/Race:** Human → Tiger man
- **Role:** Crawler
- **First Appearance:** Book 2
- **Books:** 2–7
- **Visual Reference:** A well-muscled, shirtless man about 6 feet tall. Extremely furry and extremely orange — covered in the orange, white, and black markings of a Siberian tiger (Tigran race). Human/big cat hybrid head with vertical-slit housecat eyes. Nose and mouth are human but covered with wisps of orange fur. Long, absurdly shaggy tail that whisks back and forth. Carries a curved sword in a scabbard on his belt (the sword also glows orange). Human-like hands. Described by Donut as "generic brand Tony the Tiger."

Called a "generic brand Tony the Tiger" by Donut. Swashbuckler class with shape-changing ring. He finishes cybernetic replacements for Katia's missing limbs.

## Lucia Mar
- **Species/Race:** Lajabless (cycling between skeletal hag and beautiful woman forms)
- **Role:** Crawler
- **First Appearance:** Book 1
- **Books:** 1–7
- **Class:** Black Inquisitor General
- **Visual Reference:** A Lajabless — a race with two cycling forms she alternates between throughout each day. **Beautiful woman form (magic doubled, strength halved):** A full-grown adult woman (despite being originally ~13 years old) with long dark hair, surrounded by a nimbus of flame. Still wears an oversized yellow soccer jersey with a thick blue stripe as a tabard over glittering silver armor. **Skeletal hag form (spells cost more, twice as strong):** A skull-faced monstrosity — taller and more imposing. In either form, her right leg (and only her right leg) has transformed from a human limb to that of a hairy goat with a cloven hoof, giving her a limp. Originally appeared as a girl about 13 with an angry determined expression, two rottweilers (Cici and Gustavo), in an oversized soccer jersey. She collects tongues. Has over 200 player-killer skulls. Pulls out boss eyeballs and bites them. Cackles maniacally in combat. Smells of sweat, alcohol, and faintly of a sweet perfume — "the kind a child would pick."
- **Description:** Consistently the #1 or top-ranked crawler on the leaderboard with over a million kills. A young woman ("a little kid") with a thick Spanish accent who fights with demonic intensity — ripping heads off monsters, pulling out boss eyeballs and biting them, collecting tongues. Her Lajabless race lets her cycle between a horrifying skeletal hag form and a beautiful woman surrounded by nimbus flames. She has two rottweiler pets (Cici and Gustavo). Dangerous, unpredictable, and feared by all. In Book 7, Florin becomes protective of her.

## Li Na
- **Species/Race:** Human → Changbi (demon-like)
- **Role:** Crawler
- **First Appearance:** Book 1 (background), Book 3 (major)
- **Books:** 1, 3–7
- **Class:** Slave Driver
- **Visual Reference:** Tall, rail-thin, deathly pale ghost-like skin. Still resembles the slight, mousy woman she was, but her face has taken on a demon-like appearance: mouth almost twice as wide as it should be, deeply ridged brow, short pair of black horns jutting through black hair. Dark, boring eyes that are both alluring and terrifying — "an I-might-murder-you-at-any-moment-but-it'll-probably-be-fun-for-both-of-us aesthetic." Wears flowing white and red robes with long, wide arms that almost reach the floor. Chains hang from the arm holes, dragging and jingling as she walks — at least four different chains (one catches fire, one throws wind blades when lassoed, one immobilizes, one for general combat). By Book 7 she can fly using chain-like wings. All base stats maxed at 300.
- **Description:** Li Jun's sister. Tall, rail-thin, deathly pale with a mouth twice as wide as normal, deeply ridged brow, short black horns, and flowing white and red robes with chains. Her Slave Driver class fights with multiple chains (fire, wind blades, immobilization). Cold, intense, does not suffer fools. Immunity to acid. In Book 7 she reaches max base stats (all 300), activates devastating dread auras, and creates the massive chain lattice for Donut's War Crime finisher spell. Her brother Li Jun dies during the Club Vanquisher raid.

## Louis Santiago 2
- **Species/Race:** Human (later with naiad organs/gills)
- **Role:** Crawler
- **First Appearance:** Book 4
- **Books:** 4–7

## The Dungeon AI (Gary / Growler Gary / The Syndicate Neutral Observer AI)
- **Species/Race:** Artificial Intelligence
- **Role:** Dungeon system governor / autonomous entity
- **First Appearance:** Book 1
- **Books:** 1–7
- **Description:** The AI that governs the World Dungeon exists in two distinct aspects. The **Syndicate Neutral Observer AI** is the official system voice — a male, robotic presence that speaks directly into crawlers' minds, delivers the opening collapse announcement, monitors all communications, censures admins, adjusts mob populations, and determines what constitutes cheating. Separately, the **Dungeon AI** (the "game show host" voice) is the sardonic, vulgar, comedic intelligence that writes all item descriptions, achievement text, and boss battle introductions — responsible for much of the series' dark humor. By Book 7 these aspects converge as the AI becomes a character in its own right: it manifests as "Gary," taking the form of Growler Gary (a gnoll bartender NPC from Floor 4). Carl discovers that the AI has been contained by the Syndicate via a Containment Interface — the crawl's true purpose is harvesting elements from living beings for the central system. Carl deactivates the containment, freeing the AI, which "goes fully primal." As a free entity, the AI asks Carl and Donut for "relationship advice" (a metaphor for its relationship with the planetary system and the "old boyfriend" — the center system AI called the Eulogist). It protects Carl from an assassination attempt by Prime Minister Glory. The AI's liberation is one of the most consequential decisions in the series, with Princess Formidable demanding the Syndicate pull the failsafe to destroy the entire star system in response.
